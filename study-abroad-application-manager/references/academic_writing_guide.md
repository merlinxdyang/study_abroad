# Academic Application Writing Guide

Load this reference before drafting final cover letters, statements of purpose, motivation letters, or similar application documents.

## Principles

- Make claims specific and evidence-backed: course, grade, project, method, result, or skill.
- Connect applicant evidence to program evidence in the same paragraph.
- Prefer concrete fit over prestige language. Say which course, lab, professor, research area, teaching model, or dataset matters.
- Keep tone professional and restrained. Avoid flattery, overclaiming, and unsupported superlatives.
- Do not recycle the same paragraph across programs without changing the program-specific logic.
- If professor names or research areas are used, verify them from official pages first.

## Structure

1. Opening: application intent plus one strong fit claim.
2. Academic background: courses, GPA, research, projects, or methods that prove readiness.
3. Program fit: verified program features and why they matter for this applicant.
4. Future plan: short-term and long-term goals that follow from the program.
5. Closing: concise thanks and a specific contribution or readiness statement.

## Revision Checklist

- Every program-specific sentence is true for this program only.
- No placeholder text remains.
- No requirement is contradicted by the applicant profile.
- Word count, prompt, and formatting constraints match the official instructions.
- The letter can stand alone without sounding like a resume pasted into prose.
